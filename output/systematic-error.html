Systematic Error Taxonomy:¶

TypeI: essencially statistical error(like acceptance of a detector which could be determined by other measturement which is dominated by statistics)

TypeII: like background events: we can assume a \(p(x_i|\theta)\)(and then the maximum likelihood result, \(L(\theta) = \times p(x_i|\theta)\)), here theta could be the mass of a new particle or cross-section of a given interaction, then sometime we may have background counts whichcould be represented effectively by nuisance parameter

$$\lambda$$

, so the pdf becomes \(p(x_i|\theta, \lambda)\)(and then \(L(\theta, \lamba)\)), if we assume \(\lambda\)'s pdf is gamma or lognormal(see Robert's paper, he suggest try all of the methods), we can get marginal pdf of \(\theta\) in form of maximum likelihood function. (Also you can assume prior of \(\theta\) then you are using pure baysian)
